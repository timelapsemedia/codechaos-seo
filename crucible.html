The first plugin in the Code Chaos Audio series

A harmonic saturation plugin built in the trenches of Hitech & Psycore

Crucible is a 3-band harmonic saturation plugin from Code Chaos Audio — the plugin arm of Code Chaos, one of Germany's most active Hitech Psytrance, Psycore and Darkpsy producers. It was built to solve a real problem in fast, dense psychedelic music: how do you make a track loud, warm and aggressive without turning the mix to mush?

The answer is multiband saturation. Instead of saturating the whole signal at once, Crucible splits it into low, mid and high bands so you can treat each one with its own character. Drive a Hard Clip into your sub and kick for relentless low-end punch, warm up your leads with a gentle Tube in the mids, and add Tape smear and air to the highs — all in a single instance.

Why harmonic saturation matters

Harmonic saturation adds musical overtones to a sound. Those extra harmonics make a signal feel louder, warmer and more present even before you touch a fader. Used carefully it's the secret behind tracks that translate on big festival rigs and tiny earbuds alike — and Crucible's per-band control plus up to 4x oversampling keeps that energy clean and alias-free no matter how hard you push.

Made for Psytrance, Hitech, Psycore & Darkpsy — and everything else

Crucible was forged for the fast and the dark — but harmonic saturation is universal. Drum & bass, techno, dubstep, hip-hop, rock, mastering: anywhere you'd reach for a saturator, Crucible's 3-band approach gives you more control than a single-band box ever could. Shape. Saturate. Dominate.

Crucible FAQ

What is Crucible?
Crucible is a 3-band harmonic saturation plugin by Code Chaos Audio. It splits your signal into low, mid and high bands and lets you saturate each band independently with selectable types (Hard Clip, Tube, Tape and more), per-band Drive, Mix and Gain, and oversampling up to 4x.
Is it good for Psytrance, Hitech and Psycore?
Yes — it was built by an active Hitech, Psycore and Darkpsy producer. Multiband saturation is perfect for fast psychedelic genres: add warmth and weight to kicks and basslines in the low band while adding bite and presence to leads and percussion up top, without muddying the mix.
Which OS and plugin formats are supported?
Crucible runs on Windows and macOS as a VST3 and AU plugin in all major DAWs. See the Gumroad product page for the latest supported formats and system requirements.
What's the difference between the saturation types?
Hard Clip is aggressive, loud and surgical — great for pushing low-end punch. Tube is warm, rounded and musical for mids. Tape adds glue, vintage smear and air. You can assign a different type to each band and stack them.
